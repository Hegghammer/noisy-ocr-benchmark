FIG. §7. DIAMOND DESIGN IN FLAT REED.

Fig. $7 illustrates a stool woven in flat reed. The diagonal
weave is used running toward a common center, and it forms a
diamond pattern or design, Begin wrapping on the Jong rails,
skipping every other strand on the tap as indicated. To weave this
pattern it is necessary tu locate the center of the short rails and
the center strand of those running across the frame. In this instance
the strands are ever in number, so the pattern does not begin at
the actual center, but a dittle to the right or left as the case may be.
Beginning at the center, count by twas, over, under and over, under
and so on to determine the nuber of strands to go over or under
