PART I
THE STORY OF EEAN THE FISHERMAN'S
SON
I. Thr Coxuxo of ENCHASTER
My father (eaid the youth) was a fisherman. and
he lived on this Western I-land. It may be that
be is still living here. His name was Anluan,
and he was very poor. My own name is Eean.
and the event that begins my story took place
when I was twice year of age.
My father and I had gone down to the shore of
the Western Occan. Ilc was fishing in the pools
of the and I Das putting willow rods into the
mouths of the fish caught that I might carry
them in my hands to the market that very day
and sell them there. Tooked out and saw a speck
upon the water, is speck that came I kept
watching it while ty father dragged the pool with
