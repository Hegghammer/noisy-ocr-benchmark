THE HORSES OF KIXG MANUS
It was then that the King called upon one to
finish the story that was being told him when the
white horse neighed. It was then that he sat
at the supper board, not able to take rest nor
refreshment on account of his not having heard the
story to its end. And it was then that one of the
lords said to the king, "Tet the youth who is lying
bound beside the trestles of the table tell us what
it was that made hisn go into such danger to steal
one of the horses of King Manus."
The King liked that saying, and be said, "Since
my story-leiler abides outside guarding the door
of the stable, I will have this youth tell the story
of why he entered into such danger to steal one of
my horsca. And more than that. I declare that
if be shows us that be was ever in grester danger
than he is in this night 1 shall give him his life.
But if it is not shown the story he tells will
avail him nothing. and be dall perish by the sword
at the morrow's sunrise.
