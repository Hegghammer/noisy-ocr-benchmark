THE BOY APTRENTICED TO AN EXCHANTER
Hut first the Enchanter khowed tue that great
wonder that was the gardens the Tower
of Babylos. It was a red tower mounting very
high itto the air. Outside of it there were steps
that went round it and to the very top of
it a thousand steps. And on the top of
the tower, resting agrinst the Spear of Nimpod,
was the Magie Mirror of Bebylon. Zabulun the
Enchanter made the look: to the top, and I was
made fearful by looking high.
Ohr, that I might tell you, King Manus, of the
wonders of the Tower of Babylon / In the shadow
of it there siept two mighty ones the two Geni
who guarded Babylon, Horut and Marut they were
narned. Giant beings they were As they slept
there the beard of each Was spread across his
miglity chest, and it was a beard NO broad that
no borae of the mights horses that the King owned
could leap peross it. Very great but very oll
Were Harut and Marut. the Genii who guarded
Babylon.
