HORTON
A stag's head cabossed, silver; attired, gold; and, for distinction, a canton emmine.
Crest, out of the waves of the sen proper, a tilting spenr crect, gall; cufiled with a
silver, finned, golit, and charged with a shell
[ Cabossei means cut off short soas not to show the neck; attirad denotes the borus;
means the black spots 1.pon the white field in the left-band comer. In the pleaure the
artist has not given the shell upon the dolphin.]
Motto.- QUOD VULT, VALDE VULT," Il %at ise woills, suills curdiaily and stixt.
Arms are hereditary, but the mottees are not, and may be changed to suit the taste or fancy of
any family. There are other Arms of the Torton family, varying sumewhat from the above. The
date of the grant of the arns I have Diany ceuturies ago.
